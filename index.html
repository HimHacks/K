Soyuz

The Russian Soyuz rocket, first launched in 1966, holds the record as one of the most reliable and longest-operating rockets in history. Originally developed by the Soviet Union, Soyuz has been used to launch a variety of missions, including crewed missions to space stations and satellite deployments. Soyuz was a crucial component of Soviet and later Russian space programs, enabling the Soviet Union to achieve numerous firsts in space exploration, including the launch of the first human, Yuri Gagarin, into orbit. The Soyuz rocket is known for its reliability, completing over 1,700 missions with a high success rate, making it the go-to vehicle for human spaceflight. Following the retirement of the U.S. Space Shuttle in 2011, Soyuz became the primary transport vehicle for astronauts traveling to and from the International Space Station, ensuring continued access to low Earth orbit. Its proven design and dependability make it an enduring symbol of Russian space exploration.
